# ☝️INTRODUCTION
Welcome to the generative Booth Design Add-On! This add-on is developed to enhance designers workflow in booth design by providing tools and features that could increase productivity in your design progress. 


## What do I need to know to get started?
**Basic Blender Knowledge**

Familiarity with Blender's interface and functionalities will help you get the most out of the add-on. 


## ✔️What is it good for?
**📝Base and Sketch**

Ideal for creating the foundational layout of booth designs quickly and productively. 

**📋References**

Helps in generating ideas and serves as a references during the design process. 

**💡Looking for Ideas**

Provides inspiration through its generative capabilities, allowing designers to explore various options that are unimaginable at first.


## ❌What is it not good for?

**High-Fidelity Out-of-the-Box Renders**

The generative engine prioritizes speed and viewport performance. Because of this, the default assets are low-poly placeholders. They are perfect for spatial planning but aren't intended to be the final assets users must use.

**Final Manufacturing Blueprints**

While the add-on is scale-accurate for design and pitching, it is a creative tool, not CAD software. It would be tricky for a 1:1 construction engineering or CNC-ready production files without manual verification.


## But! Don't Worry

Even though the meshes are low-poly for speed, the Camera and Lighting environments are already tuned for professional production. You don’t need to start over to get a high-quality result.

🚀 PRO TIP: Use the add-on to find the perfect composition and lighting. Once the "vibe" is locked in, swap the assets for our High-Poly Library to fo the final render.


## Features
**🎲Generative**

Quickly generates customizable booth bases that serve as the foundation for your designs. With one click, your booth would be generated only needing small adjustments and customizations.

**🪑Various Booth Elements**

Supports ideation with a variety of booth elements, including floors, roofs, walls, tables, chairs, floor elements, ceiling elements, poles, and lighting, enhancing design flexibility and creativity. Now the Add-On also supports different types of chairs, tables, floor elements, and ceiling elements allowing users to navigate through different common furnitures.

**📏Various Booth Types**

Supports ideation for various types of booths, catering to different design needs and styles, making it versatile for different projects from linear booth, end-corner booth, peninsula booth, even island booth.


## How does it work?
**🧱Generate Full Booth**

With one click, users now can generate their full booth by inputing the initial length, width, and height they want.

**🧱Generate Booth Base**

Users can specify dimensions with a maximum length and width of up to 6 meters, allowing for flexibility in design sizes. The walls are also flexible when it comes to visibility. Updated version also allow floor and roof to disappear when it reaches the height 0.

**🪑Generate Booth Elements**

Add various booth elements, including tables, chairs, floor elements, ceiling elements, and poles. The add-on randomizes their positions within the specified booth size, and the parameter is now automatically adjusted to the size of their overall booth. The add-on is now also equipped with different types of booth elements that can commonly be found in rental places.

**🎥Generate Lighting and Camera**

Choose from different lighting options to enhance the booth ambiance. The add-on also allows users to add a camera that is pre-keyframed, making the rendering process easier and more efficient. Updated version camera provides the possibility of automatic removal of roof and ceiling elements when it is positioned at the top of the Booth.


## Installation and quickstart
**🔽Download the Add-On**

Obtain the latest version from the Releases section of this repository.

**☑️Install the Add-On**

Open Blender.
Navigate to Edit > Preferences > Add-ons.
Click on Install... and select the downloaded ZIP file.
Enable the add-on by checking the box next to its name in the add-ons list.

**🔍Access the Add-On**

Locate the add-on panel within the Blender interface.
